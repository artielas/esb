8-18-2019

We are happy to announce that we have two new residents on the Board!

Elias Lancaster is a biochemist and geneticist studying a genetic mutation that runs in his family, although he claims to not have it. If you are interested, we will have a file issued on his research shortly. In the meantime, don't be afraid to introduce yourself! He's not as scary as his appearance may suggest.

Then there is Jennifer Crane, who is not a scientist. She's a theatre major at a local college, the director has chosen to let her stay with us. We have some ground rules for a non-scientist residing here:

  1. This really goes for any test subject, but if you use her as a test subject, do not use her in tests that could disfigure, injure, or kill her. Likewise, have a contract for them to sign with clearly written statements, not vague terminology. This is Eclectic Science, not Aperture Science, act like it.
  2. Do not attempt to "outsmart" her only because she's not a scientist. More traditional scientists already think of us weirdly, we do not need the general populace to do so. (This is mostly directed at Dr. Rhodes.)
  3. Please don't scare her off, she brings donuts.

Thank you for cooperating. Please act like the mature adults you are.
